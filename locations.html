7 Best Diners in St. Louis

Diner-style restaurants have been around for decades, They usually are small restaurants owned by one person or family. This makes them unique.

Youngs

youngs.com

(636) 225-6677

A family-owned restaurant providing burgers, chicken, fried seafood, pies, and soft serve since 1954.

Courtesy Diner

courtesydiner.com

(314) 644-2600

The diner was established in 1935 and shows off a classic diner look with red vinyl, checkered tile, a jukebox, and their signature dish The St. Louis Slinger.

The Buttery

thebuttery.com

(314) 771-4443

Open 24 hours the diner offers customers a place to eat early in the day or late at night. They serve breakfast at any time.

Tiffany's Original Diner

tiffanysoriginaldiner.com

(314) 644-0929

The diner offers a traditional old-time diner experience with vinyl stools and a jukebox. The menu offers a breakfast platter, hot cakes, and country-fried steak to name a few.

White Knight Diner

whiteknightdiner.com

(314) 621-5949

The White Knight Diner was featured in the movie White Palace and serves bugers, sandwiches, omelets and a slinger.

Carl's Drive-In

carlsdrivein.com

(314) 961-9652

With a history of long-term customers buying the Drive-In the traditions of the drive-in have persisted serving hot dogs, sandwiches, chili, burgers, and homemade root beer.

Southwest Diner

southwestdinerstl.com

(314) 260-7244

The diner offers more than just traditional diner food with New Mexican cooking including the breakfast tacos that started it all when the owner sold them at Tower Grove Farmers Market.
